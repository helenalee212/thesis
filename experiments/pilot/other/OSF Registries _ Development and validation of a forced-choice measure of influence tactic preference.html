Data collection

No, no data have been collected for this study yet.

We want to determine the average desirability with which each tactic is regarded.

The DV is a single-item measure of valence, asking respondents to rate the tactic from extremely positive to extremely negative.

No conditions. Participants will see a subset of the k = 96 tactics (most likely, they will see and rate 20 tactics); the tactic distribution is randomly assigned.

We will examine descriptive statistics such as mean (to determine average desirability) and SD (to determine how much raters agree on this desirability). Using a mixed-effects model, we will conduct pairwise tests to determine whether tactics that require higher capacity for volitional influence (CVI; determined by Lewis et al., 2024 analyses) are rated as more positive than those requiring lower CVI and/or higher power (again, determined by Lewis et al., 2024 analyses). We will also conduct a paired t-test (or one-way ANOVA) between the average ratings across high-CVI tactics and the average ratings across low-CVI tactics.

We will apply tests such as levers, Cook's D, and standardised deleted residuals, to identify statistical outliers. We will exclude individual observations that violate a minimum of two of these indicators. We will visually inspect for evidence of straight-line responding and other indicators of poor quality (e.g., responses to an open-ended question that say irrelevant things such as "good" or "perfect") and exclude these responses case-wise.

Sample size is based on a power analysis assuming a small effect at alpha = .05, and using monte carlo simulation (with the simR package) for a multilevel model in which tactics are nested under participants. The analysis showed that N = 273 gives 86% power and N = 311 gives 90.5% power to detect a small effect involving differences in desirability ratings of tactics. We decided to use a target sample size of N = 300.

We will collect a small sample of N = 20 and inspect the data to be sure that the study is working properly (i.e., the Qualtrics is set up correctly). We will not analyse this small sample to draw any substantive conclusions until it is combined with the full sample. In addition to the DV, we will collect demographic measures (age, gender, ethnicity, and the MacArthur Subjective Status ladder measure). We expect to conduct exploratory tests of whether perceived desirability of influence tactics is moderated by any of these measures.

Description

This is a project to develop a new forced-choice measure of preference for influence tactics. The framework is based on the Social Alignment Theory of Power (Fast & Overbeck, 2022) and the measure development approach follows Watrin et al. (2019). This registration pertains to our pre-development pilot study, testing valence/desirability of the tactics.
